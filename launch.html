Rocket travel from Earth to Mars involves several key steps:

  • Launch: The first step is to get the spacecraft off the Earth's surface, which requires a large rocket due to the planet's gravity and atmospheric drag.
  • Orbit: After launch, the spacecraft must achieve an orbit around the Earth, which is a balance of gravity and the object's tendency to follow a straight path.
  • Destination: The spacecraft must aim for Mars, which requires precise timing and direction correction to avoid missing the target.
  • Duration: The total trip to Mars takes about seven to eight months, depending on various factors such as the rocket's power, the distance between Earth and Mars, and the launch window.
